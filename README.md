# How AI Changed the Way Students Value Their Education

An interactive, self-contained web portfolio presenting a workshop study with
undergraduate Data Science & AI students — exploring how generative AI has
reshaped the way they value their education.

*Grace Nguyen · BSc Data Science & AI · University of the Arts London*
*Critical 6: Responsible Data Science — Equity, Consent, Innovation & Ethics · 2026*

## What's inside

- **Animated explainer video** of the study's design, questions, and method
- **3D neural-brain visualisation** (Three.js) — the analysis vocabulary made
  visible, with the five themes as bright anchors and the codes as glowing points
- **Interactive code map** (D3 force simulation) — 38 participant-derived codes
  that scatter away from the cursor and gather by theme when you pause on one.
  Both maps show only codes that emerged from coding what participants said;
  the study's own methodological terms (consent, GDPR, anonymisation) live in
  the write-up, not the findings — keeping the visualisations an honest map of
  the five themes
- **Full write-up**: executive summary, responsible research & innovation,
  background, method & ethics, findings, a proposed AI-literacy module,
  reflection on inclusive design, and references with hover citations

## The five themes

1. Effort and active involvement in learning
2. Authenticity, ownership and pride in human work
3. Critical engagement and the verification burden
4. Shifting perceptions of educational value
5. AI literacy as both opportunity and threat

## Run it offline

The site is fully self-contained — no internet connection required. Download or
clone this repository and open `index.html` in any browser by double-clicking it.

```
GracieNg.github.io/
├── index.html        the portfolio page (all styles + scripts inline)
├── ai_in_edu.mp4     animated explainer video
├── preview.png       social/README preview image
├── fonts/            self-hosted @font-face definitions (Fraunces, Newsreader, JetBrains Mono)
└── lib/              local copies of d3.js and three.js
```

## Ethics note

Participant evidence is **paraphrased rather than quoted** throughout this public
site, to protect the anonymity of a small cohort. The full quoted analysis is
held in the private appendices submitted to the unit leader, applying the
data-minimisation principle of UK-GDPR to publication itself.
